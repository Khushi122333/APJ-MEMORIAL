Biography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, affectionately known as the "Missile Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He hailed from a humble background but went on to become one of the most respected leaders and scientists of India.

He was instrumental in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and various missile systems, including Agni and Prithvi. His contributions to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) are unparalleled.

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007) and was fondly referred to as the "People's President." During his tenure, he inspired youth through his vision of a developed India by 2020.

He was also a prolific writer and motivational speaker, with books like "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020" that continue to inspire people worldwide.

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His legacy of knowledge, humility, and dedication to progress remains immortal.
